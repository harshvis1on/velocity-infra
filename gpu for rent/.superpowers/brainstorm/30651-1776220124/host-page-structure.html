Dedicated /host page — 6 sections

1

Hero — "Own the Infrastructure Layer"

Bold thesis statement. "In every technology era, the biggest returns go to those who own the infrastructure layer. In the AI era, that infrastructure is GPU compute." Sub-headline with yield numbers. Two CTAs: Start Hosting + Calculate Your ROI.

2

ROI Calculator / Earnings Table

Interactive or static table showing: GPU model → market rate/hr (USD) → projected monthly yield → break-even timeline (months to recover hardware cost). Shows projected annual return %. E.g., RTX 4090: $1,599 cost → $400/mo yield → 4 month break-even → 300% annual ROI.

3

"Why Compute?" — Market Context

3-4 data points making the macro case: AI compute demand growing 10x/yr, GPU shortage persisting through 2027, inference costs becoming the largest AI expense, enterprise AI budgets doubling annually. Positions hosting as investing in scarce infrastructure.

4

How It Works — 3-Step Onboarding

Streamlined: Install agent (one command) → Verify + list (auto-pricing sets optimal rate) → Earn yield (automated billing, payouts). Less technical than current page, more outcome-focused.

5

Tier Progression — "Compound Your Returns"

Reframe the XP/tier system as compounding returns. Bronze 15% fee → Diamond 5% fee = 10% more yield. Show it as: "Your effective rate increases as you level up — without changing your hardware." Visual progression bar.

6

Final CTA — "Start Generating Yield"

Closing section. "Every hour your GPU sits idle is yield left on the table." Primary CTA to /host/setup, secondary to /host/datacenter-apply for enterprise/fleet operators.

Key design decisions baked in

  • All prices in USD — landing page, /host page, and price table. Console stays INR for Indian users.
  • Investment language throughout — "yield," "ROI," "break-even," "asset class," "compound returns"
  • Hardware requirements / verification moved to docs — too technical for a marketing page
  • Security section condensed — 1 line per point instead of full cards
  • "Provider types" section dropped — replaced by the tier progression which is more compelling
